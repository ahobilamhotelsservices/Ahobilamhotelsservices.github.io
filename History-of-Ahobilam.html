History of Ahobilam Temple

Ahobilam, nestled amidst the rugged Eastern Ghats, is one of the most revered centers of Vaishnavism in India. Dedicated to Lord Narasimha, this sacred pilgrimage site is believed to be the place where the Lord appeared to protect His devotee, Prahlada.

The temple complex comprises the Nava Narasimha temples—nine shrines representing the boundless compassion and ferocity of the Lord.

  • The Chalukyas: Known for temple architecture.
  • The Vijayanagara Empire: Built towering gopurams.
  • The Reddy Kings: Local rulers who preserved the shrines.

Sacred Route Map

01

Jwala Narasimha

Garuda Bhagavan performed Tapas here. Bhagavan appeared as Jwala Narasimha and blessed the hill as "Garudaadri".

02

Ahobila Narasimha

The main deity of Ahobila Kshetram. This is where Lord Narasimha emerged from the pillar to save Prahlada (Ugra Sthambam is nearby).

03

Malola Narasimha

The gentle form of the Lord (Soumya) with Goddess Lakshmi. 'Ma' means Lakshmi, 'Lola' means beloved.

04

Kroda Narasimha

The Lord has the face of a Boar (Varaha). Worshiped to remove Rahu-Ketu doshas.

05

Karanja Narasimha

Located under the Karanja tree. Hanuman worshipped the Lord here.

06

Bhargava Narasimha

Where Lord Parashurama (Bhargava) performed penance. Located near a sacred pond (Akshaya Theertham).

07

Yogananda Narasimha

The Lord in a meditative posture. He taught Yoga to Prahlada here.

08

Chatravata Narasimha

Seated under a Peepal tree. Famous for being fond of music, as two Gandharvas sang here.

09

Pavana Narasimha

Located deep in the forest on the banks of River Pavana. Known as 'Kshetra Ratna'.
